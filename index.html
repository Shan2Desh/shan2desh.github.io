Who I am

About Me

I am an engineer who thrives at the intersection of Mechanical Design, Industrial Engineering, and Operations Excellence. My work is driven by a simple philosophy: eliminate waste, standardize what works, and build something better in its place.

My journey began in mechanical product design, where I learned how technical decisions impact manufacturing costs. Over time, my focus expanded beyond the product itself to the broader systems that create and move value. This evolution led me deep into Lean Manufacturing, fast-paced warehouse logistics, and supply chain optimization.

What makes my approach unique is this cross-disciplinary bridge. I combine the technical rigor of a mechanical engineer with the big-picture, systems-thinking mindset of an industrial engineer. Whether optimizing warehouse operations, mapping value streams, or implementing digital workflows, I look at the entire value stream rather than isolated tasks.

I approach every challenge with an owner’s mindset, focusing on how minor process tweaks drive broader strategic business goals. To me, the best solutions emerge when structured data analysis meets hands-on collaboration—whether on the shop floor, in the warehouse, or with stakeholders.

Today, my work centers on sustainable operations, process standardization, and supply chain optimization. Whether it's streamlining inventory accuracy or designing smoother operational workflows, I thrive where complexity meets execution.

Switchgear Redesign & Parts Standardization

Aug 2022 – Jan 2023 · Trainee Engineer, R&D

Situation

Switchgear assemblies used a high number of fasteners and hardware, with no shared parts library across assemblies — leading to redundant design hours and high material costs.

Task

Redesign assemblies for cost reduction and create a reusable standard parts library for the R&D team.

Action

Bosch · Nashik, India

Lean Organization in the Value Stream

Nov 2021 – May 2022 · Bachelor Thesis

Situation

The production area had excess inventory, unclear FIFO standards, and significant non-value-added search time at storage locations.

Task

Implement Lean Organization principles across the value stream to reduce waste and standardize inventory flow.

Action

Created a Value Stream Map to identify bottlenecks. Applied 5S and GEMBA at storage locations. Used Kaizen visualization for consumption control. Tracked inventory with SAP ERP (PP).

✦ Result — Reduced search time, enforced FIFO, cut excess inventory, and delivered measurable cost reductions through standardized processes.
